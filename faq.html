Getting Started
How does TideMedix work?▼
TideMedix connects you with licensed physicians for prescription weight management — all online. You complete a secure health assessment, a US-licensed physician reviews your information, and if prescribed, your medication is shipped to your door. No office visit required.
How long does the process take?▼
The health assessment takes only a few minutes. A licensed physician reviews your information after submission. If prescribed, medication ships via 2-day delivery; timing can vary based on provider review and pharmacy processing.
Do I need insurance?▼
No. TideMedix plans are self-pay and do not require insurance. Everything is included in your monthly price if eligible — physician evaluation, ongoing support, and shipping if prescribed.
Is a prescription guaranteed?▼
No. A licensed physician independently reviews your health information and makes the final determination on whether a prescription is appropriate for you. If a prescription is not approved, you will receive a full refund.
Medications & Safety
What medications does TideMedix offer?▼
TideMedix offers physician-prescribed weight management options including compounded semaglutide plans from $149/month and compounded tirzepatide plans from $249/month, if eligible. Final treatment decisions are made by a US-licensed physician based on your individual health profile.
How do I know this is safe?▼
All prescriptions are reviewed and approved by board-certified, US-licensed physicians. Medications are dispensed by accredited pharmacies. TideMedix is LegitScript-certified, which is an independent third-party verification of compliance with pharmacy and healthcare regulations. Your health information is encrypted and HIPAA-protected.
What are the potential side effects?▼
Common side effects of GLP-1 medications may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and stomach pain, particularly when starting treatment. These often decrease over time. Your prescribing physician will discuss potential side effects with you and monitor your treatment. Contact your physician immediately if you experience severe or persistent side effects.
Who should NOT use these medications?▼
GLP-1 medications may not be appropriate for individuals who are pregnant or breastfeeding, have a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, have Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2), have a history of pancreatitis, or have Type 1 diabetes. Your physician will evaluate your eligibility based on your complete health history.
